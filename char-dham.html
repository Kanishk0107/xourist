The Sacred Circuit

Four Shrines, One Soul

The Char Dham Yatra is one of the most revered pilgrimage circuits in Hinduism, encompassing four sacred shrines nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand. Xourist curates this deeply spiritual journey with the comfort, care, and reverence it deserves — ensuring your path to the divine is peaceful and profound.

The Four Sacred Dhams

Each shrine holds a unique spiritual significance

Yamunotri

The source of the sacred Yamuna River and seat of Goddess Yamuna. Trek through lush forests to reach this pristine shrine in the western Garhwal Himalayas.

Gangotri

The origin of the holy Ganga River, where the goddess Ganga is said to have first touched the earth. A breathtaking temple at an altitude of 3,048 metres.

Kedarnath

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, situated near the Mandakini River. This ancient temple stands at 3,583 metres amidst snow-capped peaks.

Badrinath

Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, this temple is one of the holiest sites in Hinduism. Set between the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges at 3,133 metres.
